Missing Demographics

Adventure Forest opens the door to everyone we can't serve today.

Young families (kids under 7) Too young for zipline. Perfect for play elements + splash zone.
The "not for me" crowd Guests who think ziplining is too adventurous, don't meet height/weight requirements, or have physical limitations.
Couples + relaxers Hot springs + food + forest walk. No adrenaline required.
Locals + repeat visitors The zipline tour is a once-or-twice experience. The swimming hole is a summer-long draw.
Existing zip guests upgrading A $40 add-on to turn a 2.5-hour visit into a half-day.

Our move: broaden the audience AND extend the visit.

The Anchor

The element that makes this a home run.

Built to look like a natural spring discovered in the woods — not a pool.

  • Boulder-edged, irregular shape, cascading waterfall
  • Clear blue-green water, stone decking, wood lounge areas
  • It has to feel like it was always there. Real stone, hand-sculpted finishes — that's where the cost goes.
  • This is the element guests will drive to, post about, and come back for
Shoulder Season Anchor

The element that keeps us open past summer.

  • Cluster of natural-looking hot-spring-style soaking pools
  • Heated, mineral-tinted, built from stacked moss-covered boulder
  • Intimate scale — 4-6 guests each — premium and exclusive feeling
  • Year-round draw: fall foliage soaks, winter couples retreats, spring warm-up
  • Extends the season and attracts a completely different guest — the couple who would never zipline but will absolutely soak in a hot spring in the mountains
Along the Loop

Small investments. Big play value. Day one.

  • Three skybridges through the upper canopy
  • Multi-level tree nets between the trunks
  • Giant bounce-net jump target
  • Stainless tube slides down a forested hillside with climbing access
  • Kid-scale playground zipline
  • Built with timber, rope, and stainless — low capex, high engagement
  • These elements ARE the loop — not add-ons

The Build

Three ways to build it.

Option A — Reid

Incremental. Annual hype cycle. Lower initial risk.

Y1 AF loop + swim hole + hot springs (3-4 pools) + play elements + 10-15 treepods + bathhouse + food
Y2 Expand swim hole + 5-6 more hot springs pools + Adventure Park + 10-15 more treepods
Y3 Wellness (yoga studio, aerial spa) + 10-15 more treepods
Y4 EverZip closed-loop + remaining treepods to 50-60
Y1 capex: $5-8M Annual "what's new" marketing story. Course-correct as you go.

Option B — Blaine

Full-scale launch. Maximum competitive moat. Find the money.

Y1-2 Build everything — swim hole, full hot springs, all play, 50 treepods, Adventure Park, EverZip, wellness
Y3 Open complete. Full marketing blast.
Total capex: $20-28M Strongest investor pitch. No half-built property. Requires external capital.

Option C — Nick

Complete at every scale. EverZip early. Expand from there.

P1 Complete but small — 10-15 treepods + AF + smaller pool + 3-4 hot springs + EverZip + ropes course
P2 Expand each element — more treepods, bigger pool, more springs, more play, wellness
P3 Full scale — 50+ treepods, full hot springs terraces, spa/wellness campus
P1 capex: $8-14M Guests get complete experience from day one. EverZip included early.

October alone is worth $1.9M.

Peak foliage. 90% pod occupancy. $550 ADR. The best month in the Smokies becomes the best month on the books.
