Editor's Note

This isn't a pitch. It's a tool to decide.

We've spent months sketching what CW Smokies could become — a swim hole, hot springs, treepods, a full destination. The vision is real. But before we commit millions of dollars to it, we need to answer one question honestly: is the Smokies actually worth the bet, and if so, how much? I think it's an incredible market to be in if we build the right thing.

A few facts make this question harder, not easier. Our zipline business is down 35% from its 2021 peak. The Pigeon Forge corridor is saturated with attractions and cabins. We're 12 miles from downtown Gatlinburg — a new product has to be worth it for people to make the drive. And lodging average day rate in our market is dropping, not rising.

But the trends are also moving toward us. Soft-adventure and wellness tourism are growing 50% faster than overall tourism. There is no commercial swim hole, no hot springs, no wellness-in-nature anywhere in the Smokies. The 13 million people who come here every year have nowhere to be in water that isn't a man-made waterpark.

This document isn't trying to sell anyone on the vision. It's trying to help us keep the Smokies conversation going because we've got to get moving in order to hit next summer. It lays out what we'd build in Phase 1 by Summer 2027 (roughly $10M — we can still add/take away), what the volume case is (backed by 275,000 of our own waivers), where we're still placeholder-ing numbers, and what would need to be true in Year 1 to commit to Phase 2.

Our mission has always been to get people outside their comfort zones in safe, controlled environments. For years that's meant adrenaline. I think we can broaden that — without losing it. Soaking in a hot spring, plunging in cold water, wading a rocky creek, going phone-free in the woods — these are all comfort-zone work for the average person. They're just a different kind. And they happen to be the kind that pulls a much larger share of who's already here.

The Drive Bar

Twelve miles from town is real.

A new product has to be compelling enough to pull a guest who's already in Gatlinburg twelve miles out — past dozens of in-town attractions. That's a higher bar than "is it cool."

Drive-out attractions in the Smokies corridor share a pattern. They're either iconic-branded, genuinely unmatched, photo-bait that earns social proof, or destination-committed via lodging:

  • DollywoodIconic brand · 3M+ visitors
  • Cades CoveThe drive is the product · 2M+ visits
  • Townsend YFree water · families willing to drive
  • Bolt Farm TreehouseLodging-committed guests
  • CW Zipline (today)Unmatched experience · 23K guests

CW Smokies' existing zipline clears the drive bar at 23,000 guests today — down from a 2021 peak of 49,000. Adventure Forest needs to clear it at 70-100K+ — meaning it has to be ~3x as compelling, or appeal to 3x as many guest segments.

The strategic frame: don't try to grow the Smokies market by luring guests who aren't already here. Win share of the 13 million who are.

The Model We're Borrowing

Adventure as a feeling,
not a requirement.

"Most of what there is to do at Anakeesta is walk, sit, eat, drink, shop — surrounded by mountain and forest views. The zipline and the coaster are optional. They sell the feeling of being adventurous without requiring much from guests to get it."

We're not trying to match Anakeesta's scale. We're borrowing the playbook: nature immersion as the product, activities as the upcharges. They've proven it works in this corridor.

What makes the model fit CW specifically: we already have the high-commitment product. A 2.5-hour zipline tour for the guest willing to gear up and go. What we don't have is the version that pulls everyone else — the family with a 4-year-old, the grandparents who don't ride, the spouse who didn't sign up for harnesses. AF is that version.

What AF adds to the model

  • Water — swim hole, real cooling in Smokies summer
  • Hot springs — the only ones east of the Mississippi
  • Year-round draw via thermal product
  • Authentic, found-feeling nature
  • Lodging path (Phase 2+)

What we're not trying to be

  • An in-town walk-up attraction (we're 12 miles out)
  • A million-visitor scale operation
  • An adrenaline park (the zipline already is)
  • A Parkway-visibility play
  • A theme park (no characters, no spectacle)

Anakeesta admission: $34.99 adult / $22.99 child / $27.99 senior. Splash Country: $54.95 / $44.95 / $44.95. AF positioning slots between these as a destination requiring a drive — but bundling water, thermal, and an EverZip ride that neither competitor offers.

Phase 1 · Summer 2027

What we build first.

The full vision is a multi-phase destination — $25.5M phased over 5 years, each phase contingent on the prior one validating. Phase 1 is the de-risked first move — $10.1M to test the destination thesis before committing the rest.

Treepods, the Adventure Park, the EverZip Mountaintop replacement, and the Wiegand-tier slide trail are all deferred. They open only if Phase 1 validates the volume case.

Phase 1 · The Elements

Nine pieces. One destination.

The Swim Hole
The anchor. Naturalistic, designed for thousands of bathers, looks found but filters conventionally. The superlative the trip is built around.
Hot Springs (4-5 pools)
Tennessee's only commercial hot springs. The year-round draw. Timed 2-hour sessions, premium add-on, the differentiator no in-town attraction can match.
Adventure Forest Hike
Loop trail with ~450 ft of canopy bridges (in-house build). Easy enough for grandparents, photo-bait throughout, the connective tissue between elements.
EverZip Final Line
Standalone ride included in AF admission — also caps off the existing Mountaintop tour. Turns the zipline business into part of AF, not a separate product.
Netted Play
Treenet Willy spec (based on our Hawaii build). 4 large nets + 2 small. The kid magnet that lets parents stop and rest while kids burn energy.
Mini Slide Park
Six commercial slides at 12-15 feet. Family-friendly scale. Designed to be Wiegand-upgradeable for monster spiral slides in later phases.
Bathhouse
Changing rooms, showers, lockers, restrooms — TN regulatory requirement for public swim. Contemporary barn architecture, designed as a place to dwell, not just transit.
Arrival Café
Walk-up window café at the entrance — espresso, breakfast burritos, pastries, smoothies. The morning energy, the photo backdrop, the place that says "you've arrived."
Smokehouse + Bar
The hero F&B venue. Cantilevered deck over the swim hole, wood-fired smoker, taps, fire pits, sunset views. Where dwell time happens. Open into evening.
Deferred to Phase 2+

Adventure Park (non-belayed). Treepods (15→50 over 4 years). EverZip MT Course replacement. Wiegand-tier slide trail. Wellness/spa. Full hot springs buildout to 8-10 pools.

The Trigger

What tells us to go bigger.

Phase 1 is the test. Year 1 Summer (Jun-Aug 2027) AF visitor count is the binary signal that drives the treepod commitment for 2028.

35K+
GO

Summer 2027 visitors hit 35K. The 70K+ annual run-rate validates the volume thesis. Commit to full 15-pod Phase 2 treepod buildout for 2028.

20-35K
CONDITIONAL

Volume is there but soft. Build a 5-pod pilot in 2028 to test the lodging-conversion thesis. Defer full buildout to 2029.

<20K
HOLD

Volume is below the floor. Debrief — marketing, product, pricing, location. No treepod capex until we understand the gap.

Why these thresholds: Nick's plan = 175 guests/day × 92 summer days ≈ 16K just for summer (out of 36K annual). 35K summer = ~70K annual run-rate, which is the volume thesis. 20K summer = the volume floor where the premium thesis still works.

Capital · The Honest Part

Smokies can't carry this alone.

Phase 1's $10.1M is roughly 2-3x the Smokies business's annual EBITDA. The full $25.5M is 5-6x — but it's spread over 5 years, with each phase contingent on the prior one. Some combination of bank debt + cash flow + outside equity is the reality — not solved in this document.

PATH A · BANK

Construction-to-permanent loan. 70-75% LTV. CW puts up $6-8M equity (cash + land value). Rate ~7.5-8.5%. DSCR clears 1.25x minimum even in Year 1; comfortable 4.5x at steady state. Standard path. Local Smokies-corridor banks know tourism.

PATH B · EQUITY PARTNER

Investor capital for 25-50% equity. 15-25% IRR expectation. Possible multi-property structure (Smokies + other CW assets) for portfolio diversification. More expensive capital but shares downside risk; complicates governance permanently.

Default recommendation: bank path, given the strong DSCR coverage and CW's operating history. Path B opens up if CW can't put up the equity, or if there's strategic value in a multi-asset partner. This is a Kolby + lender conversation, not a brainstorm conclusion.
